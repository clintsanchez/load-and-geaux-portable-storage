# Load and Geaux Brand Style Guide

A concise guide to the Load and Geaux brand voice, visual identity, and usage standards.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Brand Personality
**Friendly + Professional + Locally Rooted**

Load and Geaux speaks to Baton Rouge customers as a trusted local partner—professional enough for business customers, approachable enough for homeowners, and deeply connected to Louisiana culture.

### Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Sound knowledgeable and confident about portable storage solutions
- Be conversational and helpful (not stuffy or corporate)
- Use local references and cultural touchstones (Louisiana, Baton Rouge, Bayou)
- Focus on customer benefits (ease, reliability, peace of mind)
- Show personality and warmth in communication
- Use active voice and clear, direct language
- Ask questions to understand customer needs
- Provide specific, useful information

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate jargon or overly formal language
- Make vague promises or hype claims
- Sound generic or regional (this is a LOUISIANA brand)
- Overuse technical shipping/container terminology without explanation
- Be condescending or talk down to customers
- Use slang excessively (balance "geaux" attitude with professionalism)
- Make it about us; make it about their needs

### Example Tones

**Website Headline (Good):**
"Whether you need space for a home renovation or emergency storage, Load and Geaux has the perfect container solution—fast delivery, honest pricing, zero hassle."

**Website Headline (Poor):**
"Portable storage solutions tailored to customer requirements and logistics optimization."

**Social Media (Good):**
"Storm damage? Home project? Just need extra space? We've got you covered with climate-controlled containers delivered right to your Baton Rouge home. Fast. Reliable. Geaux."

**Blog Intro (Good):**
"If you've ever wondered whether a 16-foot or 20-foot container is right for your project, you're not alone. This guide breaks down the real differences—and helps you choose what actually fits your space and budget."

## Visual Identity

### Logo & Wordmark

**Primary Logo:** Load and Geaux horizontal logo with optional Louisiana state silhouette  
**Files:** `brand/logos/load-geaux-horizontal-logo-*.png` (various scales and colors)  
**Formats:** 
- Green version (primary): `load-geaux-horizontal-logo-green.svg/png`
- White version (for dark backgrounds): `load-geaux-horizontal-logo-white.svg/png`
- Compact square version: `load-geaux-square-logo-green.png`

**Usage Rules:**
- Always include adequate whitespace around logo (minimum 20px on all sides)
- Never stretch, skew, or rotate the logo
- Use the white version on dark backgrounds, green on light backgrounds
- For small sizes (<100px), use the simplified version
- Avoid using as a repeating background pattern

### Typography

**Headline Font:** Bold Sans-Serif (or modern serif)  
- Should feel confident and strong
- Examples: Inter Bold, Poppins Bold, or Georgia Bold
- Sizes: 32px+ for main headings, 24px for subheadings

**Body Font:** Clean Sans-Serif  
- Should be highly readable at small sizes
- Examples: Inter, Segoe UI, -apple-system, sans-serif
- Sizes: 16px for desktop, 14px for mobile
- Line height: 1.5-1.6 for readability

**Accent/Brand Font:** Optional serif accent for quotes or testimonials

### Imagery Style

**Photography:**
- Real product photos (containers in actual customer scenarios)
- Warm, natural lighting when possible
- Shows containers in use (driveways, worksites, businesses)
- Customer lifestyle images (people, real projects)
- Local Baton Rouge landmarks and scenery

**Graphics & Infographics:**
- Data visualizations should be clear and educational
- Use brand colors as accent colors
- Include relevant statistics and sources
- Design should look professional, not playful

**NOT Acceptable:**
- ❌ Clip art or hand-drawn SVG illustrations of real objects (containers, trucks)
- ❌ Overly stylized cartoon imagery
- ❌ Stock photos that don't feel authentic
- ❌ Unbranded generic storage imagery

**Use Instead:**
- ✅ AI-generated images from client's actual photos (via Gemini/Claude image gen)
- ✅ Real product photography from portfolio
- ✅ Professional lifestyle photography
- ✅ Data-driven infographics with brand colors

## Writing Standards

### Headline Style
- **Sentence case** for most pages (capitalize only first word and proper nouns)
- **Benefit-focused** (emphasize what customer gets, not company feature)
- **Action-oriented** for CTAs (Use "Get started," "See our containers," "Request a quote")
- **Specific over vague** (Use "16-foot vs 20-foot storage comparison," not "Size options")

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Climate-controlled storage for peace of mind"
- ❌ "Climate Controlled Storage Services"
- ✅ "Find the right container for your project"
- ❌ "Portable Storage Solutions"

### Body Copy Style
- **Short paragraphs** (2-3 sentences max, especially for web)
- **Subheadings** to break up long content
- **Bullet points** for lists and features
- **Active voice** whenever possible
- **Simple words** (avoid jargon without explanation)
- **Second person** (You/Your, not "customers" or "clients")

**Examples:**
- ✅ "You need reliable storage for your renovation project, and we deliver."
- ❌ "Customers seeking storage solutions are encouraged to contact the company."

### Call-to-Action (CTA) Copy
- **Direct and benefit-clear:** "Get a free quote," "See pricing," "Explore sizes"
- **Action verb at start:** "Discover," "Learn," "Get," "Schedule," "Request"
- **Avoid:** "Click here," "Submit," "Process," generic/vague phrasing

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Get your free quote today"
- ✅ "Schedule a container delivery"
- ❌ "Click to continue"
- ❌ "Submit your information"

### Blog & Content Writing
- **Titles:** Use benefit-first, searchable keywords naturally
- **Intros:** Hook with a question or relatable scenario
- **Structure:** Use short paragraphs, descriptive subheadings, bullets
- **Conclusion:** Tie back to customer benefit or offer next step (CTA)
- **Tone:** Educational and helpful, not salesy

**Blog Post Framework:**
```
Headline (Benefit + Keyword)
↓
Hook/Intro Question (Relatable scenario)
↓
Subheading + Brief intro
↓
3-4 main sections with subheadings
- Short paragraphs with bullets or emphasis
- Mix of explanatory text + practical examples
↓
Conclusion (Recap + CTA)
↓
CTA Section (Quote/Schedule/Contact)
```

## Social Media Guidelines

### Posting Tone
- Slightly more casual than website, still professional
- Use Louisiana/local references and humor
- Customer-focused, not company-focused
- Share useful tips, not just promotions

### Content Mix
- 50% Educational/valuable (tips, guides, how-tos)
- 25% Community/local (local events, customer stories, Baton Rouge mentions)
- 15% Promotional (offers, services, calls-to-action)

## Email & Marketing Copy

### Email Headers & Subject Lines
- **Length:** Keep under 50 characters for mobile readability
- **Benefit-first:** Lead with what customer gets
- **Curiosity:** Use numbers ("5 Container Sizes You Didn't Know About") sparingly and honestly
- **Personalization:** Use customer name when available, natural and not forced

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Your custom storage solution is ready"
- ✅ "Baton Rouge homeowner? Storage just got easier"
- ❌ "Important: We have an exciting announcement"

### Email Body Copy
- **Conversational:** Write like you're talking to a friend
- **Short:** Especially first paragraph (2-3 sentences max)
- **Links:** Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here"
- **CTA:** Single, clear primary action per email

**Example Email Opening:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Thanks for choosing Load and Geaux for your storage needs! Your 
20-foot container is scheduled for delivery tomorrow between 
8am-noon.

[Here's what to expect →]
```

## Design System & Components

### Button Styles
- **Primary CTA:** Forest Green background, white text, rounded corners
- **Secondary:** White background, Forest Green border and text
- **Hover state:** Slightly darker green or increased opacity
- **Disabled:** Gray background, muted text

### Form Fields
- **Border:** Light gray (#F3F4F6)
- **Focus:** Forest Green border, soft shadow
- **Labels:** Dark gray text, optional red asterisk for required fields
- **Placeholders:** Light gray, hint text style

### Cards & Content Blocks
- **Background:** White or very light gray
- **Border:** Subtle light gray or soft shadow
- **Padding:** Comfortable whitespace (24-32px)
- **Accent bar:** Optional Forest Green top or left border

## Brand Usage Don'ts

**Never:**
- ❌ Change logo colors or proportions
- ❌ Use outdated logo versions (v1 or v1.5)
- ❌ Place logo too close to content without whitespace
- ❌ Use non-brand colors as primaries
- ❌ Copy brand voice as generic corporate speak
- ❌ Use stock imagery that looks inauthentic
- ❌ Make claims about speed/quality without specifics
- ❌ Use the name as "Geaux" alone (it's "Load and Geaux")

## Quick Reference

### For Headlines
Use: Benefit + Specificity + Local Connection  
Avoid: Generic, vague, or overly formal language

### For Body Copy
Use: You/Your, active voice, short paragraphs, simple words  
Avoid: We/Our (except for brand stories), passive voice, jargon

### For Color
Use: Forest Green for primary elements, white for backgrounds, steel gray for text  
Avoid: Multiple accent colors, low-contrast text

### For Imagery
Use: Real product photos, AI-generated from client photos, professional lifestyle photography  
Avoid: Clip art, hand-drawn SVGs, overly stylized or generic imagery

### For Social
Use: Local references, educational content, authentic customer stories  
Avoid: Sales-focused posts, generic business jargon, hashtag spam
